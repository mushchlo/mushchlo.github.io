Gravimetric Analysis

Jan MacGregor - Oct. 20th, 2020

Purpose

  In this lab, a container with a partially damaged label was given, which read that it had the formula XCl2 (X being the part scratched out, it was two characters), indicating that the element X was an alkaline earth metal. The intent of the following lab was to determine the unknown element, and thus the full formula of the salt, using gravimetric analysis.

Background Info

  There is an direct relationship between the mass of a substance and the number of atoms in that mass, but it is dependent on the substance, more specifically the substance's "molar mass". This, along with the fact that when reacting a substance with another, the resulting molecules have different molar masses than when they began, means that with careful weighing, some algebra, and examination of the possible chemical equations, the identity of a substance can be determined with high certainty, which is what was done in this lab.

Procedure

  • In order to find a relationship between the mass of the unkown substance and the molar mass (which would be used to identify it), the unknown was reacted with silver nitrate, which when mixed with any chloride salt in solution, would precipitate out silver chloride. The reaction would be as follows (regardless of which alkaline metal X is, as they all have the same charge):

    XCl2 (aq) + 2AgNO3 (aq) → X(NO3)2 (aq) + 2AgCl (s)
  • Before anything else, a watchglass was tared on the scale, with nothing on it and the door to the scale closed. It was left in the scale until needed.
  • Once the solutions were mixed, a cloudy white precipitate formed (the silver chloride), which was then poured off into a vaccuum filter, and the pump turned on. After the precipitate had turned into a cake from the filtration, it was placed on the watchglass that was in the scale, which was then placed into the drying oven for 30 minutes, along with dessicant.
  • After 30 minutes, the dried precipitate on the watchglass was placed back on the scale, and came out to __g of silver chloride.
